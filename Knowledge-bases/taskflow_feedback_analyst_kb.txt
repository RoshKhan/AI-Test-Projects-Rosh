[INT-001]
Date: 2026-04-05
Participant: Sarah K. | Role: Operations Manager | Segment: SMB
Company size: 18 employees | Plan: Pro | Tenure: 8 months
Interviewer: UX Researcher

Interviewer: "Thanks for joining us Sarah. Can you start by describing how your team uses TaskFlow day to day?"

Sarah: "Sure. We're a logistics coordination company, so we're managing anywhere from 30 to 50 active jobs at any time. My team is split — some people are office-based and use desktop, but my field coordinators are almost entirely on their phones. That's where we've had the most friction."

Interviewer: "Tell me more about that friction."

Sarah: "The mobile app just doesn't keep up. My field team needs to update task statuses, attach photos as proof of delivery, and check dependencies on the fly. The photo attachment feature crashes at least once a day. The dependency view isn't even available on mobile. And if they're in an area with poor signal, anything they do offline just disappears. It's caused real problems with our clients."

Interviewer: "How has that affected your team's adoption of TaskFlow?"

Sarah: "Three of my field coordinators have basically stopped using it. They WhatsApp me updates and I enter them on desktop. That defeats the purpose entirely. I've been a TaskFlow advocate internally but I'm running out of arguments to make."

Interviewer: "What would make the biggest difference for you?"

Sarah: "Honestly? Reliable offline mode and photo uploads that don't crash. Those two things would change everything. The desktop product is great. The mobile product feels abandoned."

Interviewer: "How was your initial onboarding experience?"

Sarah: "That was also rough. We signed up on a Friday afternoon and got an automated welcome email. No one reached out until I submitted a support ticket two weeks in. By that point we'd already configured things incorrectly and had to redo a lot of setup. A 30-minute kickoff call on day one would have saved us weeks."

[INT-002]
Date: 2026-04-12
Participant: Marcus T. | Role: Director of PMO | Segment: Enterprise
Company size: 1,200 employees | Plan: Enterprise | Tenure: 2.5 years
Interviewer: UX Researcher

Interviewer: "Marcus, you've been with TaskFlow for over two years. What's your overall view of the product today?"

Marcus: "Overall still positive but I have concerns. The core platform is excellent — portfolio views, resource allocation, reporting. Our PMO relies on it deeply. But we're seeing a growing disconnect between the desktop experience and what our mobile users get."

Interviewer: "Can you be specific about that disconnect?"

Marcus: "My project managers who are desk-based love it. But our team leads who are on-site — manufacturing floor, client sites — they're getting a significantly worse experience on mobile. The executive dashboard doesn't render on mobile, Gantt charts are read-only on the app, and we had a sync issue last month where mobile edits weren't reflecting on desktop for up to 15 minutes. In a fast-moving environment that's a real problem."

Interviewer: "Has this affected your renewal consideration?"

Marcus: "We're early in our renewal cycle but yes, it's a topic. My CTO has asked me to do a formal platform review and mobile capability is on the evaluation criteria. TaskFlow needs to show us a credible mobile roadmap or we'll have to consider alternatives. We've been a loyal customer and I don't want to switch, but the gap is widening."

Interviewer: "What about onboarding? You onboard new teams regularly."

Marcus: "That's my other major pain point. We add new departments to TaskFlow every quarter. Every single time it's the same story — three weeks of setup, confusion about permissions, people not knowing how to use templates. There's no enterprise onboarding playbook. No white-glove service despite our contract size. We've had to build our own internal training materials. TaskFlow should own that."

Interviewer: "If you could send one message to the TaskFlow product team what would it be?"

Marcus: "Invest in mobile as if it were a first-class product, not a companion app. And create a proper enterprise onboarding programme. Those two things would secure our renewal."
